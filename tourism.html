Explore the Wonders of Leiden

Leiden is home to stunning landmarks, each with its own unique story. Whether you're exploring historic museums or taking a walk by ancient windmills, you'll fall in love with this city.

Molen De Valk (The Falcon Windmill)

One of the most iconic symbols of the Netherlands, De Valk is an authentic 18th-century windmill. Located in the heart of Leiden, this windmill is not only a picturesque site but also a museum where visitors can learn about the history of grain milling.

  • Built in 1743
  • Standing at 29 meters tall
  • Museum open to the public

Hortus Botanicus Leiden

Established in 1590, Hortus Botanicus Leiden is the oldest botanical garden in the Netherlands. Wander through lush greenery, exotic plants, and historic trees, and explore glasshouses filled with rare species from around the world. A perfect escape into nature within the city.

  • Founded in 1590
  • Includes rare plants like the Titan Arum
  • Perfect for family outings and photography

Pieterskerk

The Pieterskerk, dating back to 1121, is an architectural masterpiece that holds centuries of Leiden's history. The church played a significant role in the city's religious and academic development, and it was the burial place of many prominent figures. Today, it serves as a historical site and hosts various events throughout the year.

  • Built in 1121
  • Burial place of many famous Dutch figures
  • Used for exhibitions and concerts
